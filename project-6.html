Project 06

Healthcare Patient Portal

AccessibilityAngular WCAG 2.1UX Research

Mar 2022 — Jul 2022

Project Overview

Designed and built an accessible patient portal for a regional hospital network serving 120,000 patients. The portal had to meet WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards, support patients aged 18–85, and integrate with existing FHIR-compliant medical record systems.

Challenge

The patient population included a significant proportion of elderly users (38% over 65) and users with visual or motor impairments. The legacy portal had a System Usability Scale score of 38/100 — classified as "poor" — and phone call volumes to the appointments line remained high.

Solution

Ran usability studies with 18 participants across three age groups, including 6 participants with disabilities. Implemented high-contrast mode, scalable typography up to 200%, keyboard navigation throughout, and full screen reader compatibility. Reduced appointment booking steps from 8 to 3.

Tech Stack
AngularTypeScript FHIR APIaxe-core
Year2022
RoleAccessibility Engineer

Key Outcomes

  • System Usability Scale score improved from 38 to 82 — classified as "excellent"
  • Appointment phone call volume reduced by 44% within the first month of launch
  • Zero WCAG 2.1 AA violations on final accessibility audit — first portal in the network to achieve this
